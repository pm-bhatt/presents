## 1. Purpose

Sangyaa builds identity and access management tooling to help organisations operate more securely and efficiently. We believe that technology built to verify *who* a person is and *what* they can access carries a particular responsibility — because the same tooling that protects legitimate users can, if misused, be turned against them.

This AUP exists to make clear the categories of use that Sangyaa does not permit, under any licence, commercial or otherwise.

## 2. Prohibited uses

You may not use, deploy, integrate, or make the Software available — directly or indirectly, in whole or in part, modified or unmodified — for, on behalf of, or in material support of any of the following:

### 2.1 Weapons and military applications

- Development, production, testing, deployment, maintenance, or operation of weapons systems of any kind, including conventional, chemical, biological, radiological, or nuclear weapons.
- Nuclear weapons programmes, including enrichment, weaponisation, delivery systems, or command-and-control infrastructure for nuclear arsenals.
- Autonomous weapons systems or lethal autonomous targeting.
- Military intelligence, combat, or battlefield operations.
- Organisations whose primary business is the manufacture or sale of weapons or munitions.

This clause does not prohibit use by civilian government agencies for non-weapons purposes (e.g. a transportation ministry, a public health department), provided no other clause of this AUP is violated.

### 2.2 Mass surveillance and violations of civil liberties

- Mass surveillance programmes targeting general populations rather than specific lawful investigations.
- Systems designed to suppress, identify, track, or retaliate against journalists, activists, dissidents, protestors, whistleblowers, political opposition, religious minorities, ethnic minorities, LGBTQ+ individuals, or other groups exercising fundamental rights.
- Social credit scoring systems that restrict access to services, employment, housing, travel, or civic participation based on political views, associations, or lawful conduct.
- Biometric surveillance in public spaces without clear legal basis, meaningful consent, and independent oversight.
- Any use that contravenes the Universal Declaration of Human Rights, the International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights, or equivalent regional human rights instruments.

### 2.3 Harm to minors

- Any system, service, or process that facilitates the sexual exploitation, abuse, trafficking, or grooming of children.
- Generation, distribution, or facilitation of child sexual abuse material (CSAM) in any form, including synthetic or AI-generated material.
- Targeting of children under 16 with manipulative, addictive, or exploitative design patterns.
- Use in contexts where children are subjects of experimentation, surveillance, or profiling without parental consent and independent ethical review.

### 2.4 Harm to animals

- Industrial-scale factory farming operations that violate recognised animal welfare standards.
- Trophy hunting, poaching, or illegal wildlife trade.
- Animal testing for cosmetics or non-essential commercial products.
- Organisations whose primary activity causes systematic, avoidable animal suffering.

This clause is not intended to restrict use by veterinary services, accredited zoological or conservation organisations, ethical agricultural operations, or legitimate medical research conducted under established ethical oversight.

### 2.5 Unlawful and harmful activity generally

- Any activity that is unlawful under the laws of the United Kingdom, the European Union, India, or the jurisdiction in which the Software is deployed.
- Fraud, money laundering, sanctions evasion, or financing of terrorism.
- Human trafficking, forced labour, or modern slavery.
- Unauthorised access to computer systems, malware development, ransomware operations, or other cyber-offensive activity that is not sanctioned security research conducted with proper authorisation.
- Discrimination against protected classes under applicable equality law.
